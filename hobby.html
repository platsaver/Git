Three types of switches:

  1. Linear:

    2. Linear switches have the simplest operation, moving straight up and down without any additional tactile feedback or loud clicking noise

  2. Tactile

    3. Tactile switches provide additional tactile feedback as the key actuates.

    As you press the key down, there is a noticeable bump which lets you know that your key press has been registered.

    This GIF will demonstrate how tactile switch works:

  3. Clicky

    4. Clicky switches add a deliberately louder ‘click’ sound to the existing tactile bump, allowing for greater typing feedback.
